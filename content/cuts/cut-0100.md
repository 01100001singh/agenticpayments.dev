In 2017 the RBI's Master Direction required full KYC for prepaid wallets. The industry had built a hundred-million-user business on minimum-KYC onboarding — a mobile number and a name. When the deadline hit, wallets that hadn't converted users to full KYC watched their active bases collapse. Some lost the majority of transacting users in months. The money was still there; the right to hold it was not.

The lesson operators took away was not "do better KYC." It was that regulatory risk isn't tail risk in payments — it's the main distribution. A rule change is not a black swan; it is weather. Every business model that depends on a permission — to hold balances, to skip an authentication step, to price a service at zero — should be valued with the assumption that the permission gets repriced.

Agentic wallets, allowances, and platform credit balances all live on permissions like these. Most of their builders haven't been through weather yet.
