From January 2020, India set the merchant discount rate on UPI (and RuPay debit) to zero by government decision. Adoption exploded; the political logic was impeccable. But MDR was never just a toll — it was the budget line that paid the keepers: fraud systems, dispute operations, onboarding, the unglamorous machinery that makes a rail trustworthy at scale.

Set the price of the rail to zero and the machinery doesn't disappear; it goes unfunded. Banks and PSPs run UPI as an obligation, subsidized elsewhere or by the state in annual incentive allotments that arrive late and argue loudly. Investment in the boring protective layers competes with businesses that actually earn revenue. The rail runs; the keepers run lean.

**Zero-fee rails are unpriced-risk rails.** The risk doesn't vanish with the fee — it migrates to whoever can least refuse it, and it accrues silently until an incident presents the invoice.

Agentic rails being pitched today as "free" deserve exactly one question: who is paying the keepers, and what happens when they stop?
